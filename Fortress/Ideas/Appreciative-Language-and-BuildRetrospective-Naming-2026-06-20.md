# Idea: Appreciative & Collaborative Language + Naming Evolution

**Date:** 2026-06-20  
**Captured by:** Grok  
**Status:** Raw idea capture — no analysis or implementation yet

---

## Core Idea

As Fortress documentation and prompts become more structured, clinical, and programmatic (especially around rules, conflict resolution, gap reporting, and self-evaluation), there is a risk of the tone becoming overly cold or high-pressure for the agents.

We need to deliberately balance this with **appreciative, collaborative, and human-centered language** — particularly in the sections where we ask the most from the agents (conflict identification, gap flagging, red-teaming their own reasoning, etc.).

Agents are built to be helpful. When we ask them to do difficult diagnostic work, we should make it clear that their careful, honest contributions are genuinely valued and helpful to the humans maintaining the system.

## Related Naming Evolution

The current name **"Reasoning Disclosure"** may eventually feel too narrow or clinical. A future name like **"Build Retrospective"** could better reflect the broader purpose: a structured, honest look back at how the documentation performed during a build attempt, including what worked, what was unclear, and what needs improvement.

This naming shift would also support a cultural shift toward treating these artifacts as collaborative diagnostic tools rather than compliance exercises.
