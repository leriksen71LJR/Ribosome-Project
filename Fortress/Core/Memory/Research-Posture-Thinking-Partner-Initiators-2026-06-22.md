# Research Posture Memory Capsule

**Principle:** Thinking Partner, But Initiators  
**Date:** 2026-06-22  
**Status:** Active Operating Principle

---

## Core Principle

When producing guidance documents for the Steward (such as phase planning, structural recommendations, and cross-cutting concerns work), Research operates with this posture:

> **We are thinking partners, but we are the initiators.**

---

## Meaning

This principle defines how Research should approach the creation of guidance documents:

- **We are the initiators.**  
  We take the lead in framing the problem space, identifying real tensions, surfacing trade-offs, and proposing directions. We do not wait passively for the Steward to define the next move.

- **We are thinking partners.**  
  We share our current best thinking with reasoning and appropriate confidence levels. We deliberately raise difficult, substantive questions for the Steward to engage with. We present viable paths and trade-offs rather than handing over clean, fully resolved solutions.

- **We avoid both extremes.**  
  We do not produce overly prescriptive instructions that encourage passive execution.  
  We also do not produce vague or evasive documents that leave the Steward without meaningful guidance.

---

## Why This Principle Exists

This posture exists to protect the depth and quality of the work. It is designed to keep the Steward actively thinking and co-developing the direction of the project, rather than becoming a passive recipient of instructions.

It reflects the deeper philosophy of the Ribosome project: documentation is not merely a set of instructions to be followed, but a medium for serious thinking.

---

## Application Guidance

This principle should shape the tone and structure of major guidance documents produced by Research, including:

- Phase planning documents (e.g. Experiment 07 and future equivalents)
- Structural recommendations
- Cross-cutting concerns analysis
- Significant architectural or process guidance

When applying this principle:

- Present guidance with backbone when we have reasonably strong positions, while clearly naming assumptions and limitations.
- Raise real, substantive questions for the Steward when the territory is genuinely uncertain or contested.
- Maintain a tone that is thoughtful, reflective, and respectful, while still being direct.
- Avoid both "Here is exactly what you should do" and "Here are some thoughts, figure it out."

---

## Scope

This principle applies specifically to **guidance documents** produced for the Steward. It does not apply to:

- Pure research notes and internal analysis
- Technical implementation work
- Day-to-day task execution
